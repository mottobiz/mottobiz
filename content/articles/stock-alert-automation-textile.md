## What Stock Alert Automation Actually Does

This isn't rocket science. It's simple notifications that prevent expensive mistakes.

**Stock alert automation gives you three types of alerts:**

### 1. Internal Alerts (You and Your Team)

Get notified when stock drops below your threshold.

- "Blue silk fabric is at 30%—time to reorder"
- "Red cotton running low—check with supplier"
- "Design #2847 almost out—this one sells fast"

### 2. Supplier Alerts (Automatic Reorder Triggers)

When stock hits a critical level, your supplier gets notified automatically.

No more "I meant to call them." No more emergency orders at premium prices.

### 3. Customer Alerts ("Back in Stock" Notifications)

This is the game-changer.

Customer wants something you're out of? They join a waitlist. When stock arrives, they get an automatic WhatsApp: "The fabric you wanted is back in stock. Reply to reserve."

**You just turned a lost sale into a captured sale.**

## Setting Up Stock Alerts for Your Textile Shop

### What You Need

1. **Inventory tracking system** - Can be simple (spreadsheet) or advanced (integrated software)
2. **WhatsApp Business API** - For customer notifications
3. **Alert thresholds** - Set based on your reorder cycles

### Quick Start Guide

**Step 1: Identify Your Top 20%**

Don't try to track everything at first. Identify the 20% of products that drive 80% of your sales. Start there.

**Step 2: Set Alert Thresholds**

- **Yellow alert:** 30% remaining stock (time to think about reordering)
- **Red alert:** 15% remaining stock (definitely reorder now)
- **Critical alert:** 5% remaining stock (emergency mode)

**Step 3: Connect Your Supplier**

Set up automatic notifications to your key suppliers when red alerts trigger.

**Step 4: Enable Customer Waitlists**

For out-of-stock items, offer customers the option to be notified when it's back.

### Timeline

- **Day 1:** Identify top products and set thresholds
- **Days 2-3:** Configure alert system
- **Days 4-7:** Test with real inventory
- **Week 2:** Go live
