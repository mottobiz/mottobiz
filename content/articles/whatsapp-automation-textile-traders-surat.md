## What is WhatsApp Automation?

Let's clear up a common misconception first.

**WhatsApp automation isn't a chatbot that annoys your customers.**

It's a system that handles the routine stuff—the "what's your price for 500 meters of this fabric?" inquiries—so you can focus on the conversations that actually close deals.

Here's what it does:

- **Captures every inquiry** automatically (no more lost leads buried in chat history)
- **Responds instantly** with relevant information based on what they asked
- **Qualifies leads** so you know which ones to call personally
- **Follows up** on your behalf when leads go cold

<ProTip>

**Insider tip:** Most Varachha traders we work with start by automating price quote requests only. That single use case saves 3-4 hours per week and responds to inquiries in 30 seconds instead of 4 hours.

</ProTip>

What it doesn't do:

- Replace your personal relationships (those still matter)
- Handle complex negotiations (it flags those for you)
- Sound robotic (when done right, customers can't tell)

Let me break down each one.

### 1. Order Inquiry Responses

This is where most Varachha traders start.

When someone messages "Do you have silk fabric in blue? Need 200 meters," your automation:

1. Recognizes they're asking about stock
2. Checks your inventory (if connected)
3. Sends availability + price range
4. Offers to send samples or arrange a call

All in under 30 seconds. While you're having lunch.

### 2. Price Quote Requests

"How much for 500 meters of cotton fabric?"

Instead of typing the same quote 20 times a day, your automation:

1. Asks clarifying questions if needed (quality grade, delivery location)
2. Sends your price range instantly
3. Offers to confirm exact pricing within X hours
4. Logs the lead for your follow-up

### 3. Stock Availability Checks

If you connect your inventory system, customers can check stock in real-time.

"What colors do you have in fabric code XYZ?"

The system responds with actual current stock. No back-and-forth while you check your register.

### 4. Follow-up Sequences

Here's where a lot of deals happen.

Customer asked for a quote but didn't respond? The system follows up:

- Day 3: "Did you get the quote? Any questions?"
- Day 7: "Still interested? We have a new shipment coming in"
- Day 14: Final check-in before moving to long-term nurture

<Warning>

**Common mistake:** Don't make follow-ups sound like pressure sales. Keep them helpful: "Just checking if you had questions about the quote" works better than "When are you placing your order?"

</Warning>

### 5. Payment Reminders

Awkward to chase payments? Let automation handle it.

Gentle reminders at 7 days, 14 days, 30 days—customized to your relationship with each customer.
