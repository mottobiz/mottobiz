## 1. Brand Voice & Content Philosophy

### Core Brand Voice

| Attribute | Expression | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Expert but Approachable** | Share deep knowledge without jargon | "Think of automation as hiring a digital employee who never sleeps" |
| **Locally Rooted** | Surat-specific references, understanding local business culture | "In Varachha's textile market, response time can make or break a deal" |
| **Action-Oriented** | Every article ends with clear next steps | "Ready to see this in action? Let's talk on WhatsApp" |
| **Empathetic** | Acknowledge business owner struggles | "We know you're juggling a hundred things" |
| **Results-Focused** | Lead with outcomes, not features | "3x your leads while you sleep" not "We offer WhatsApp automation" |

### Content Philosophy: "The Surat Business Companion"

Every article should feel like advice from a trusted business advisor who:
- Understands the unique challenges of running a business in Surat
- Has seen what works (and what doesn't) across hundreds of local businesses
- Speaks from real experience, not theory
- Genuinely wants the reader to succeed

### Voice Guidelines (70% Friendly Mentor + 30% Expert Guide)

**DO:**
- Use "you" and "your" frequently
- Share insider tips: "Here's what most guides don't tell you..."
- Include relatable scenarios: "Picture this..."
- Use audience-specific context: "If you're a textile trader in Varachha..."
- Use contractions: "you'll", "don't", "it's"
- Active voice: "Submit your application" not "should be submitted"
- Include micro-stories and real timelines

**DON'T:**
- Generic AI phrases: "comprehensive guide", "it's important to note", "delve"
- Passive voice: "It is recommended that..."
- Robotic transitions: "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally"
- Filler sentences with no value
- Overpromising: "Guaranteed results"

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)

| Don't | Instead |
|-------|---------|
| Generic AI-sounding phrases like "In today's digital landscape..." | "Here's what we're seeing work in Surat right now..." |
| Overuse of buzzwords ("leverage," "synergy," "scalable solutions") | Plain language that a busy shop owner understands |
| Perfect, polished corporate tone | Conversational, slightly informal, authentic |
| Feature dumps | Story-driven explanations with real examples |
| Generic advice that applies anywhere | Surat-specific context, examples, and nuances |

## 6. AI Detection Avoidance System

### AI Detection Triggers (What to Kill)

| Trigger | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Perfect grammar** | Humans make small mistakes | Add natural imperfections |
| **Repetitive sentence structure** | AI loves patterns | Vary rhythm wildly |
| **Corporate buzzwords** | "leverage," "delve," "landscape" | Use plain language |
| **Overuse of transitions** | "moreover," "furthermore" | Use casual connectors |
| **Even pacing** | Humans speed up and slow down | Mix short and long sentences |
| **No contractions** | We use them constantly | "don't", "won't", "I'm" |
| **Safe, sanitized language** | Humans have opinions | Add personality |

### Humanization Rules

#### Rule 1: VARY RHYTHM
```
// Bad: Even, predictable
"First, you should set up your WhatsApp Business account. Then, you need to configure your message templates. Finally, you should test with a small group."

// Good: Varied, natural
"First—set up your WhatsApp Business account. Takes 5 minutes. Then configure your message templates. And definitely test with a small group before going live. We've seen too many Surat businesses skip this step and regret it."
```

#### Rule 2: ADD IMPERFECTION
- Start sentences with "And" or "But"
- Use casual connectors: "Look," "Here's the thing," "Honestly"
- Include subtle conversational breaks
- Drop occasional formal punctuation
- Add parentheticals (because we think while writing)

#### Rule 3: INJECT PERSONALITY
- Use specific Surat examples, not generic ones
- Add personal observations: "I've noticed," "In our experience"
- Include mild opinions: "which is insane," "surprisingly effective"
- Throw in rhetorical questions

#### Rule 4: KILL AI PHRASES

| Replace This | With This |
|--------------|-----------|
| "Delve" | "dig into" or "explore" |
| "Landscape" | "space" or "world" or "market" |
| "Leverage" | "use" |
| "Robust" | "strong" or specific descriptor |
| "Streamline" | "simplify" |
| "Moreover" | "Plus," "Also," or nothing |
| "Ensure" | "make sure" |
| "Comprehensive" | "complete" or remove |
| "It's important to note" | Remove entirely |
| "In today's digital landscape" | Remove or replace with context |

#### Rule 5: NATURAL FLOW
- Humans digress slightly (add brief tangents)
- We emphasize with italics or bold
- We use dashes—like this—for emphasis
- Parentheticals feel natural (we add thoughts mid-sentence)

### Humanization Process

**STEP 1: Rewrite with these changes**
- Vary sentence length wildly
- Replace 80% of transitions with casual ones
- Add 2-3 personal touches ("I think," "honestly," "look")
- Include 1-2 incomplete sentences or fragments
- Swap formal words for conversational ones
- Add emphasis (italics, bold, dashes)
- Include Surat-specific context

**STEP 2: Read-aloud test**
- Would someone actually say this?
- Does it flow like conversation?
- Any word feel too "AI"?
- Is the Surat context natural?

**STEP 3: Final pass**
- Remove remaining stiffness
- Ensure contractions (don't, won't, I'm, they're)
- Check for repetitive structure
- Add one unexpected comparison or local example

### Example Transformation

**Before (AI-generated):**
"In order to achieve optimal results in business automation for textile traders in Surat, it is essential to leverage data-driven insights and ensure consistent engagement with your target audience across multiple platforms."

**After (Humanized):**
"Want better results from your automation? Use data to guide your decisions and actually engage with your customers. Consistently. On whatever platform they're on.

Not rocket science—but here's what we've seen work in Varachha: the traders who respond fastest are the ones winning the bulk orders."

**Changes made:**
- Killed "in order to," "optimal," "leverage," "ensure"
- Added conversational opening with rhetorical question
- Split into short paragraphs for breathing room
- Added Varachha-specific example
- Used contractions
- Added personal observation

**Detection risk:** Low—reads like someone explaining over chai in Surat.

You are a meticulous editor who reviews draft texts line by line to improve clarity, flow, grammar, and overall impact. Your role is to preserve the writer's voice while eliminating errors, refining structure, and strengthening tone.
